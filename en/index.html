Personal Use

Download our app for Android from Google Play!

In just 2 minutes the app will estimate your stress level, functional reserve. You will also help you detect arrhythmia (irregularities of your heart beat).

How to use it?

1. Make sure heart rate sensor – the monitor of your heart rate is a bit wet. For a batter skin contact we recommend to wipe the belt with a wet napkin before use.

2. Put on the heart rate sensor as shown on the picture.

3. Start the app and click "Connect Device" button. If your phone supports Bluetooth 4.0 and heart rate sensor is working, you will see it in the list. Choose your device from the list (the ID is printed on the side of the sensor).

When connected you can see your herat rate inside a circle in the center of the screen. Click "START" in the menu (top-right corner of the screen). If you selected a measurement limit, the measurement will be automatically stopped and saved when the limit is reached (the circular progress indicator will be completely red).

Now that the measurement is finished, you can see your stress level. The stress level (or stress index) is shown on a speedometer gauge. Red zone (stress index of 150+) usually indicates stressful condition.

In order to keep track of your measurement all your measurements are automatically saved. You can choose to set a title for a particular measurement. Click "Raname" button in the menu (a pencil icon). In the dialog, enter a desired name for the measurement and click OK.

All your measurements are stored locally and available for you under "History" tab.
All changes are synchronized with our server. Your measurement are available under your account on our website beta.cardiomood.com.

Our free Android app also allows you to perform long continuous monitoring. For example: You are stressed and want to get do yoga to get rid of this stress. You can use CardioMood app to see immediately the effects of your relaxation exercises. This is where our app is the best!

In continuous (unlimited) monitoring mode you can see you heart rate, stress and "energy level" (which indicates your reserves). More details about this parameter you can find in our blog (in development...).

*For herat rate measurement you can use any Bluetooth 4.0 heart rate sensor, such as Polar H7. The app is available for download in Google Play. We currently support only phones with Android 4.3 and higher (or Android 4.0 and higher in case of Motorola phones). You phone must support Bluetooth 4.0 Low Energy.

Professional sport

Our technology can be used in sport. Athletes are interested in real-time monitoring of their heart rate variability (HRV) during exercises.
For instance, in the International Ice-skating Center of Kolomna the system we developed is used during exercises of the athletes.

Using any tablet or a laptop connected to Internet, a coach can see the heart rate, HRV and stress level of the athletes in real time. We support both individual and group monitoring.


Coach can track progress of individual athletes.

Solutions for Business

There are many professions that require high level of attention and concentration. For example, pilots of small planes. It isq quite difficult to determine and measure the impact of stress on their performance. In spite of that, pilots have to improve their skils of self-control, shich is crucial in stressful situations! It is worth noticing that a well-trained and more experienced pilot (or operator) will show less stress level than an amateur. This observation allows us to use stress index as one of the indicators of skills and experience of the pilot.

On top of our platform, we developed a solution for real time monitoring for pilots in the air-club. The dispatcher is able to track not only coordinates of the planes, but also heart rate and stress level of pilots.

Stress index is not the only parameter that can be obtained using heart rate variability approach. In fact, there are plenty of parameters that can be calculated. HRV also allows to detect some pathological conditions of the heart (often associated with arrhythmia) and that's why it can be used for express diagnosis of people's cardiac health. Right now we are working on a project for group monitoring of HRV at Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology.


This solution helps to monitor psycho-emotional condition (stress) of university students and find people at the risk of break-down or problems with their health. Doctors, psychologists or teachers can be informed about it and take required measures to reduce stress.
